Going above and beyond to exceed the expecations of the client is why I chose to develop my 3D design skillset. Learning how to texture, animate and model in Blender has greatly expanded my creative toolkit to be a more versatile designer.

CLIENT PROJECTS

PROJECTS OVERVIEW

My 3D visualisations have been a critical component in securing contracts with Harrods, Tottenham Hotspurs, and Celebrity clientele. Being able to generate scenes from architectural sketches and photographs goes above and beyond client expectations and allows them to experience their concept in physical space. NDA's limit the information that can be shared to protect private client anonymity.

CHALLENGES

Managing client expectations for image revisions is an important step in delivering projects to strict deadlines. Remodeling bespoke items can be time-consuming, so our teams deliver sketches for the client to approve before any 3D design is undertaken.

REFERENCE MATERIALS

Photographs from clients or on-site teams, sketches from designers, and architectural drawings are common for previsualisation projects. For projects such as Tottenham, the 3D model was generated by an in-house CAD team that I textured and animated.

Securing a BID with Harrods was my first professional 3D design project. By visualising the area of the Technology Centre that the cabinet would be located the Harrods team could better understand the scale, materials and design before committing to the project.

COMMERCIAL PROJECTS

OVERVIEW

Releasing a rollable outdoor cinema required a brochure that would be placed in retail stores alongside the product to explain the features and customisation options. 

I finalised the design and functionality of the cabinet in Blender before generating a series of product and lifestyle renders to complete the brochure design.

CHALLENGES

As the product was still in the concept stage I had to collaborate closely with the Matthew Hill Bespoke design team and the Future Automation technical director to ensure the final design would be practical to construct and on budget.

The deadline for this project necessitated a design sprint to deliver the physical brochures in time for the product release. Understanding the production timeline to receive the run of brochures was critical in projecting deadlines to key stakeholders.

The Cineair release was the first commercial project I worked on where no physical product had been completed prior to the delivery of the marketing materials. Relying solely on 3D design to deliver every product and lifestyle image was a challenging prospect that I overcame throughout the delivery of this project.

REFERENCE MATERIALS

Faithfully representing bespoke materials was achieved by having physical samples at my desk as I worked. This ensured I could communicate additional material properties (specular highlights, UV gloss coatings, texture) that were hidden in photographs.

The lifestyle scenes were achieved in a short timeframe by utilising a stock house model that I textured and lit. Additional plants and textures were also used to bring life to the scene.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

An important sales tool for B2B clients is the 'Sell Sheet'. Incorporating 3D design into my workflow enabled me to deliver product and lifestyle renders of bespoke product concepts that would be impractical and costly to build and photograph.

This helped reduce the pressure on sales teams accurately communicating complex products using wireframe CAD models.

CHALLENGES

Conceptualising realistic scenes in which to place products presents a difficult challenge at scale. However, many objects and materials can be reused across multiple projects to reduce workload.

Animating complex products requires close communication with mechanical engineering teams to ensure all aspects of the product are functioning correctly.

REFERENCE MATERIALS

Untextured models for products are delivered as STL/STEP files. These need to be separated into their individual components, grouped, textured, and animated.

PRODUCT VISUALISATIONS

TECHNIQUE

I discovered in my experimentation that choosing stock textures from online resources will repeat across large surfaces. Creating texturing using noise layers as a base (imagine tv static) allows me to scale and warp materials to infinitely scale without repeating.

Assigning a bump texture based on whether the noise is black or white gives the texture an accurate 3d depth to catch the light properly. 

Finally, the colour of the texture is applied to the black and white textured surface. The colour is assigned to the bumpy surface depending on how far the texture is from the surface of the object.

Creating the textures in this way allows them to be easily transferred between objects that are drastically different scales. These are stored in my own custom library for future projects.

CHALLENGES

Online material swatches often misrepresent metallic/reflective surfaces making them impossible to recreate digitally without an artistic touch.

Custom materials are often intricately detailed and require multiple creative effects to be accurately represented.

REFERENCE MATERIALS

Physical texture samples are always preferred, but image references are often used for shorter deadlines. Having years of experience capturing photographs and videos of a wide selection of projects for case studies means I have an invaluable library of reference materials to refer to while creating new textures. 

I'VE REPLACED MUCH OF MY PRODUCT PHOTOGRAPHY WITH BLENDER. IT ALLOWS ME TO CREATE IMAGES OF LARGE ITEMS THAT ARE IMPRACTICAL TO PHOTOGRAPH, OFFERS THE ABILITY TO CHANGE PRODUCT COLOURS/MATERIALS, AND GIVES ME MORE CREATIVE CONTROL OVER LIGHTING.
