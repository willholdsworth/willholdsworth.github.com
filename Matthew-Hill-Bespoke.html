I exceeded internal and external stakeholder expectations following the acquisition of Matthew Hill Bespoke by Future Sound & Vision. This was achieved by generating compelling graphic design, photography and written content. Learning to use Blender also allowed me to incorporate 3D design into my workflow for the first time. This new skills gave the business development team the additional content they needed to pitch to celebrity clientele and Fortune 100 businesses.

Marketing Design:

LITERATURE & IMAGERY

Inspiration for the brochure came from modern galleries with stark white walls, descriptor cards and compelling imagery. 

High-quality images of completed projects were identified as a key area to attract new clients and were a vital component of the brochure. 

Negotiating access to completed projects ensured I could deliver a varied catalogue of photographs and videos for marketing purposes moving forward.

These materials empowered the sales team with the tools required to pursue luxury clientele with confidence.

Online Strategy:

WEBSITE
REDESIGN

I created a new online presence for the business so that potential clients could browse previous projects, view factory tours and contact the sales team directly.

The site utilises a clean and minimal design, leaning heavily on quality imagery and video. This creates a rich viewing experience for potential clients.

Using an online site builder ensured additional strain was not added to the Future Sound & Vision web development team. 

This approach also ensured that Matthew's team would be able to manage the website after the initial design was completed without any prior design experience.

Bids/Tenders:

HARRODS KINETIC
MEDIA CABINETRY

Incorporating 3D design into my workflow enabled our team to submit bids for Harrods Technology Centre.

I created renders based on initial concept sketches and photographs from the Matthew Hill team. These were incorporated into pitch document that outlined the scope of the project for Harrods to receive ahead of the pitch.

Visualising the project in this way was critical to the projects' success as the Harrods interior design team were able to visualise the physical size and material finishes available for the cabinetry.

The accompanying pitch document was also designed to be reformatted as a sales brochure for the Harrods team to present to their clients once the media cabinet was installed in the technology centre.
