I specialise in creating minimalist combination marks that form a strong foundation on which to build a memorable physial and digital brand identity.

FUTURE AUTOMATION

INSPIRATION

The main icon is inspired by an off-axis display that mimics the movement of the products that Future Automation manufacture.

I was also inspired by modular logos that adapt to the platform that they are being viewed. This is why the icon and lettermark can appear separately or together to best suit the context in which they are being used.

FUNCTIONALITY

Icon can be laser cut into metalwork without losing interior lettering shapes. The lettermark can be stitched into staff uniforms for tradeshows and events. The icon is ideally suited to digital applications (social profile images) and can be customised with lifestyle imagery to give more context to the business.

PROJECT SCOPE

I was headhunted by Future Sound and Vision to deliver a company rebrand for Future Automation. The redesigned logo is a small part of a much larger branding excercise. The new logo better embodies the products that Future Automation makes and allowed the company to expand its online presence with a 'digital first' flat and minimalist design. 

Additional icons were created for specific services with a matching aesthetic to create a cohesive identity for the brand.

SHADING PRO

INSPIRATION

Distilling 'shadow' into a simple, yet recognisable form required multiple design revisions to achieve the minimalist icon in this combination mark.

The serif font mirrors high-fashion magazine covers whose customers share many of the same values as the luxury designers and architects that Shading Pro was also targeting.

I was inspired by the fabric weave found in many of the materials that were being implemented into the shades. Traditional 'cross-hatching' drawing techniques influenced the central horizontal lines to 'shade' the surrounding 'sun' which sits at the heart of the logo.

FUNCTIONALITY

The icon can be discreetly heat-stamped into fabric to create a distinct, yet subtle mark to identify Shading Pro shades from competitors. As all Shading Pro products are wide and thin, the logo is presented in a landscape format so that it can be printed onto products and packaging without needing to be manipulated.

PROJECT SCOPE

The newly formed company needed a complete 'ground-up' brand identity exercise to shake up the already established blinds/shading industry. After identifying interior designers and architects as the target market for the product range I tailored the logo and marketing materials to appeal to this demographic.

Being a newcomer to the industry allowed me to tailor the look and feel of the brand to separate it from key players in the industry who are less agile in their design approach.

FUTURE SOUND & VISION

INSPIRATION

The original logo remained unchanged since 1998, so I wanted the new design to draw inspiration from the original mark. 

The form encapsulates the audio-visual industry, combining a pupil with sound waves.

The music video 'Cymatics' introduced me to Chladni Plates which are a way of visualising different sound frequencies using a black metal plate. I distilled and refined the plate into the illustrated 'pupil'.

FUNCTIONALITY

When scaled the icon reduces into more simplistic forms as the 'waves' so the logo remains identifiable on small devices/prints. The lower lettermark can be separated from the combination mark for stitched clothing or laser etching.

PROJECT SCOPE

The Future Sound & Vision logo design was a part of a larger branding exercise to reestablish Future Sound & Vision as the parent company to its subsidiaries. This created a clearer distinction between the house of brands as the company grew, allowing for more concise pitch meetings, presentations and acquisitions.

MATTHEW HILL BESPOKE

INSPIRATION

Isometric drawings are common in furniture design CAD models. I took this design style to remap the Matt Hill initials into the shape of a table to echo the furniture design aspect of the business.

Other initial designs were inspired by wood grain and negative space. 

FUNCTIONALITY

This refreshed logo aimed to visually separate the company from similar furniture-makers who had tailored their branding to match Matthew Hill Bespoke. The modernised logo avoids sans serif fonts that are common among competitors and an easy-to-understand visual language that appeals to interior designers.

PROJECT SCOPE

Following the acquisition of Matthew Hill Bespoke our team recognised the need to deliver a series of marketing materials. The owner chose to include a logo redesign to inform the rest of the branding exercise.
